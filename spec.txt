Here is the functional feature set and usage guide for your Life Operating System, built on Vercel and Supabase.

1. High-Level Goals

Centralized Data Warehouse: A single source of truth for financial, physical, professional, and digital health.

Split-Personality Interface: A UX designed for two distinct modes of life: Doing (Mobile) and Analyzing (Desktop).

Growth Mindset: Visualizations focused on upward trends, volume accumulation, and consistency streaks rather than punitive metrics.

Correlative Insights: Ability to see how behavior in one area (e.g., phone usage) affects performance in another (e.g., gym consistency).

2. Usage Patterns

Mobile Experience (Data Entry):

Acts as a collection of "Mini-Apps" or bookmarks on the home screen.

Optimized for speed: Large buttons, auto-calculations, and simple sliders.

Context-aware: Specific URLs for specific environments (e.g., opening the Gym module only when at the gym).

Desktop Experience (Analytics):

Acts as "Mission Control."

High-density dashboards, complex charts, and historical data review.

Used for weekly reviews and long-term planning.

3. Feature Modules
A. Financial Health

Goal: Track wealth accumulation and net worth growth.

Features:

Net Worth Dashboard: A stacked visualization showing the ratio of Cash vs. Investments over time.

Hybrid Data Sync: API integrations for automatic bank syncing, with a manual override interface for specific assets.

Income vs. Expense: Monthly cash flow tracking to monitor savings rate.

Asset Breakdown: Granular view of specific stock portfolios vs. liquid cash.

B. Gym & Physical Health

Goal: Consistency and progressive overload (Volume/Strength).

Features:

Workout Logger: Mobile-optimized interface to log Sets, Reps, and Weight in real-time.

Volume Calculator: Automatic calculation of total tonnage lifted per session.

PR Tracker: Historical log of "Big Lift" maximums (Bench, Squat, Deadlift, etc.).

History Lookup: "Last time you did this" reference visible during data entry.

Consistency Streak: Binary tracking of workout days vs. rest days.

C. Career & Student Life (CS Major)

Goal: Academic success and professional skill acquisition.

Features:

Dynamic "Buckets": Create and archive tracking buckets for specific classes, research labs, or personal coding projects.

Dual Tracking: Option to track by time spent (Timer/Duration) or by completion (Binary "Done").

Consistency Heatmap: GitHub-style visualization showing daily activity across all study/career domains.

Project Logs: Space to attach notes or milestones to specific study sessions.

D. Digital Wellbeing

Goal: Conscious technology usage.

Features:

RescueTime Integration: Automated background syncing of device usage.

Category Split: Visual comparison of "Productive Time" (VS Code, Docs) vs. "Distracted Time" (Social Media, Streaming).

Platform Breakdown: Analysis of time spent on Mobile vs. Desktop.

E. Daily Review & Metacognition

Goal: Qualitative analysis and daily reflection.

Features:

EOD Wizard: A guided mobile flow to wrap up the day.

Subjective Metrics: 1-10 Sliders for "Mood," "Energy," and "Perceived Success."

Journaling: Short-form text entry for "Wins" and "Improvements."

Tagging System: Quick-select tags (e.g., "Tired," "Motivated," "Social") to correlate feelings with data.

F. Analytics & Visualization

Goal: Identify spikes and correlations.

Features:

The "Spike" Chart: A master line graph overlaying major categories (e.g., Gym Volume vs. Study Hours) to see peaks in performance.

Radar Charts: "Balance of Life" visualization to see if one area is being neglected.

Growth Trends: Long-term trend lines for Net Worth and Lift Strength.

Correlation Engine: Visuals that help answer questions like "Does high phone usage correlate with lower study hours?"
